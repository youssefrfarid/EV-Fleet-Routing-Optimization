\section{Problem Formulation Overview from Literature}
The reviewed studies typically define the EVRP as a complex, multi-faceted optimization problem. The general goal is to find a set of vehicle routes that minimizes a primary objective, such as total operational time or total energy consumption, while adhering to numerous constraints. The formulation must balance routing decisions with vehicle-specific limitations, most notably the EV's battery capacity, which necessitates strategic stops at charging stations. A recurring challenge is the accurate modeling of the charging process itself, with recent literature favoring non-linear functions that reflect real-world battery behavior. Furthermore, when multiple vehicles are involved, the problem expands to include fleet coordination, especially when resources like charging stations are limited or shared, introducing the possibility of queues and waiting times.
